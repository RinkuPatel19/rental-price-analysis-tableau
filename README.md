## Overview
This project analyzes rental price trends across the United States from 2010 to 2017 using Tableau. The goal is to explore regional differences, seasonal patterns, and long-term trends in rental pricing to support data-driven decision-making for renters, investors, and policymakers.

## Problem Statement
Rental markets are dynamic and influenced by factors such as location, population, and seasonality. Understanding these patterns is essential for:
- Renters making housing decisions  
- Real estate investors identifying opportunities  
- Urban planners improving housing strategies  

This project uses visual analytics to uncover key trends in the U.S. rental market.

## Key Insights
- Washington, DC has the highest average rent (~$2,400/month)  
- California and New York consistently show high rental prices  
- Midwest and Southern states offer more affordable housing  
- Rental prices peak during summer and decline in winter  
- Strong correlation exists between population size and rent levels  
- Michigan shows steady growth with future upward trends  

## Business Impact
- Helps investors identify high-demand rental markets  
- Supports pricing strategies based on seasonal trends  
- Enables urban planners to optimize housing strategies  
- Assists renters in making cost-effective housing decisions
